Months and Days

The Months

January

Based on the name of the latin god Janus (guardian of portals, doors, and gates)

February

`Februa/Februum` means `purification`

March

Based on the name of the latin god Mars (god of war)

April

The origin is not clear, some theories say it's based on Aphrodite's name, other say it's based on a word that means `the next one`.

May

Based on the name of the latin goddes Maia (goddess of the earth)

June

Based on the name of the latin goddes Juno (goddess of the adult women)

July / Quintilis

Originally "the fifth month", renamed to Iulius after the death of Julius Ceaser

August / Sextilis

Originally "the sixth month", renamed to Augustus after the death of Augustus Ceaser

September

The seventh month (actually the ninth)

October

The eighth month (actually the tenth)

November

The nineth month (actually the eleventh)

December

The tenth month (actually the twelveth)

The Days

Romans named the days of the week based on the 7 planets...

Monday

"Moon's day", from the latin "Lunas Díes" (or "Lunes" in Spanish)

Tuesday

Based on the germanic god Tiu (germanic version of Ares, god of war)

In latin it was "Martis Díes" ("Mars' day"), and "Martes" in Spanish

Wednesday

"Woden's day", "Woden" is a variant of the name "Odin", nordic god of a lot of things

In latin it was "dies Mercurii" ("Mercury's day"), and "Miércoles" in Spanish

Thursday

"Thor's day", nordic god of thunder.

In latin it was "dies Iovis" ("Jupiter's day"), and "Jueves" in Spanish

Friday

Two theories: "Frigga's Day" (germanic goddess of love) or "Freya's Day" (nordic goddess of love)

In latin it was "Veneris dies" ("Venus' day"), and "Viernes" in Spanish

Saturday

"Saturn's day", roman god of agriculture and time

In latin it was "dies Saturnii"

In Spanish it's "Sábado" from the latin "Sabbatum" which means "Rest" (like the shabat in Hebrew)

Sunday

"Sun's day", from the latin "dies solis"

In Spanish it's "Domingo", which means "The day of the Lord"
